The nightlight
that fights back.

Neevel is an end-to-end fall prevention system — sensor-equipped insoles paired with a nightlight-shaped hub that coaches older adults through daily balance exercises. No smartphone required.

2nd place 249 submissions 33 countries 8 finalists

The problem

Every year, nearly 700,000 people die from falls worldwide.

Older adults are the most affected — yet many of these falls are preventable. Studies show that consistent balance exercise reduces fall rates by 24%, and three hours per week can push that number to 42%.

The tools built to help them — smart insoles paired with smartphone apps — miss a basic point. They feel clinical, inconvenient, and dependent on digital habits many older adults do not want to adopt. Prevention becomes a burden instead of a routine.

Fall prevention should not require a smartphone. It should feel less like a device and more like care, dignity, and routine.

How it works

A loop that fits into the end of a day.

01
Wear
Sensor-equipped insoles capture pressure, stability, and gait patterns linked to fall risk throughout the day.
02
Dock
At day's end, the insoles dock into a nightlight-shaped hub to charge and sync — no phone, no pairing, no setup.
03
Coach
The hub coaches daily balance exercises through voice and light — prevention embedded into an existing routine.

The team

Two students. One thesis.

Neevel was built across two continents by an industrial designer and a computer engineer who believe useful products should feel desirable — even the hard ones.

Verônica Vanti
Industrial Design · Product
Industrial design student and Neevel's design lead. Verônica redesigned fall prevention from a symbol of decline into an object older adults actually want in their homes — quiet, warm, and giftable.
Andrei Vince
Computer Engineering · Systems
Computer engineering student building the sensing, firmware, and data systems behind Neevel — sparse pressure telemetry, on-device inference, and the hub that turns raw signal into a daily coaching loop.
